The cycle most learners never escape

If you've been learning Japanese for a while and still feel stuck, you've probably been through this more than once:

  • 1
    Find a new method A YouTube channel, an app, a textbook that someone swears by.
  • 2
    Start with momentum It feels right. You're consistent for a few days or weeks.
  • 3
    Lose traction Progress slows. The method stops feeling effective.
  • 4
    Blame the method "Maybe this isn't the right approach for me."
  • 5
    Search for a better one And the cycle starts again. Sometimes for years.

The method was never the problem.

Every popular method probably works

Anki. Immersion. Shadowing. Textbooks. Conversation partners. Apps.

The learners who succeed with each of these aren't more talented. They didn't find the "right" method. They stopped asking "is this the right method?" and started asking "how do I make this work for me?"

The method is not the variable.

Your relationship with learning is.

A method that seems inefficient for most learners can be exactly right for you — if it keeps you in contact with the language. And a "proven" method can completely fail if you don't understand what it's actually building.

That's the question I want to help you answer.
